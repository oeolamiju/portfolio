Statistical Analysis of Birthrates and influencing Factors
Across Europe and Asia using RStudio

By identifying the drivers behind birth rate variations, policymakers can develop targeted interventions to promote health and achieve sustainable population growth. The main objectives of this research are as follows
I. Evaluate the significance of relationships between birth rates and associated factors across Asia and Europe. II. Analyse how birth rate trends change over time across the two regions. III. Check whether Asia and Europe's average population growth and fertility rate are the same. IV. Build models that can predict future birth rates in the two regions combined.

Machine Learning (Classification and Clustering Algorithms) Projects Using Databricks and Jupyter Notebooks

The first project outlines a data analysis task involving fault classification in a manufacturing machine using vibration sensor readings. Two binary classification algorithms, Decision Tree and Random Forest, were employed, and their performance was compared using a customer dataset from an Iranian Telecom company. The goal is to recommend an efficient model for identifying customers likely to churn. The task follows the CRISP-DM methodology. Additionally, two clustering algorithms, DB-SCAN and K-Means, were used to build models estimating obesity levels in individuals from Mexico, Peru, and Colombia based on eating habits and physical conditions. The clustering models aim to identify clusters in the dataset and analyze their performance in estimating different levels of obesity across individuals.
